About Me

I am a graduate student at Paris Diderot University (Paris 7), in the second year of the Parisian Master of Research in Computer Science (MPRI) curriculum. I graduated from the ENSEA graduate school in electrical engineering and computer science in 2014.

Research Interests

I am interested in the domains of programming languages, formal verification, computer architectures, and operating systems, as well as their applications in the field of computer security.

Current Work

I currently work at Inria under the supervision of Cătălin Hriţcu. I study how micro-policies, a general framework for expressing hardware-assisted security monitors, could help in designing efficient secure compilers. A secure (or fully abstract) compiler preserves the abstractions of the source language when translating a program, it thus allows the programmer to reason about security properties at the source language level.
